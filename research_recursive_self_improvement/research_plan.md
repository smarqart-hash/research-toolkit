# Forschungsplan: Recursive Self-Improvement in AI Systems

## Hauptfrage
Welche theoretischen Grundlagen, empirischen Grenzen und Implikationen hat recursive self-improvement in AI-Systemen — insbesondere fuer Knowledge Work Automation?

## Subtopics (5 parallele Recherche-Straenge)

### 1. Theoretische Grundlagen (Semantic Scholar + Web)
- Recursive self-improvement, intelligence explosion, seed AI
- I.J. Good (1965) bis aktuelle Alignment-Forschung
- Formale Modelle: AIXI, Goedel machines, self-modifying agents

### 2. Empirische Grenzen & Ceilings (Semantic Scholar + Exa)
- Self-play limitations, reward hacking, mode collapse
- LLM self-evaluation accuracy, self-correction failures
- Goodhart's Law in AI optimization pipelines

### 3. AI-Assisted Knowledge Work (Exa + Web)
- LLM impact on research workflows, literature review, analysis
- Productivity studies (GitHub Copilot, legal AI, scientific writing)
- Deskilling vs augmentation debate

### 4. Self-Referential & Reflexive AI Systems (Semantic Scholar)
- AI Scientist (Sakana AI), STORM, OpenScholar
- Self-improving code generation (AlphaCode, self-debugging)
- Meta-learning, AutoML, neural architecture search

### 5. Epistemologische Implikationen (Web + Exa)
- AI-generated knowledge reliability
- Epistemic automation, model collapse from training on AI output
- Human-AI hybrid epistemology
