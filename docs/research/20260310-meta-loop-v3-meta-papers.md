## Synthesis: Five Key Meta-Findings

### 1. **Evaluation Crisis**
No standardized benchmarks for evaluating AI research tool quality. Existing evals measure chat quality (GAIA, CUB), not research integrity. **Gap**: Need PRISMA-style evaluation rubrics for automated research pipelines.

### 2. **Hallucination as Systemic, Not Random**
Hallucinations are mathematically traceable failure modes (intrinsic vs. extrinsic), not rare bugs. 100+ hallucinations in NeurIPS 2025 papers prove institutional defenses failed. **Implication**: Peer review cannot detect AI-hallucinated citations at scale.

### 3. **Human-AI Synergy Rare, Conditional**
Meta-analysis shows human-AI teams perform *worse* than best individual in 70% of cases. Synergy emerges only in content creation where AI executes, humans direct. **For research**: Complementarity requires role clarity (human: synthesis, AI: retrieval).

### 4. **Epistemological Paradigm Shift Required**
Classical epistemology (empiricism, falsification) breaks under AI. Need three new frameworks: (a) pragmatic computational empiricism (utility + safeguards), (b) adversarial epistemology (human-machine co-evolution), (c) democratic AI epistemology (accountability). **Ceiling**: Without new epistemology, AI research tools risk producing computational authority without epistemic legitimacy.

### 5. **Reproducibility as Active Process**
Reproducibility cannot be post-hoc verification. Future: automated capture of research artifacts (dead ends, hyperparameters, versions) via native audit trails. **For this toolkit**: Provenance logger + JSONL audit trail aligns with this direction.
