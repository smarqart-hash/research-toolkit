## Core Literature Review & Knowledge Synthesis Tools

### 1. STORM: Synthesis of Topic Outlines through Retrieval and Multi-perspective Question Asking
- **Authors:** Yijia Shao et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Venue:** NAACL 2024 (arXiv:2402.14207)
- **Key Finding:** LLM-powered Wikipedia-like article generation through simulated multi-perspective conversations; 25% improvement in organization vs. baseline retrieval-augmented approaches
- **Citation Count:** High (Stanford publication, flagship system)

### 2. Co-STORM: Human-AI Collaborative Knowledge Curation
- **Authors:** Stanford OVAL Lab
- **Year:** 2024
- **Venue:** EMNLP 2024 (main conference)
- **Key Finding:** Extends STORM with human-in-the-loop collaborative discourse protocol; shared conceptual space representation (mind map UI) for interactive knowledge refinement
- **Citation Count:** Medium (newly accepted, integration of knowledge-storm v1.0.0+)

### 3. Language Agents Achieve Superhuman Synthesis of Scientific Knowledge (PaperQA2)
- **Authors:** FutureHouse Team (includes Blair Ding et al.)
- **Year:** 2024
- **Venue:** arXiv:2409.13740 (September 2024)
- **Key Finding:** RAG agent achieves superhuman precision (85.2%) on literature research tasks; matches/exceeds SME performance without human restrictions; 21.9% explicit "insufficient information" detection
- **Citation Count:** High (superhuman performance benchmark)

### 4. OpenScholar: Synthesizing Scientific Literature with Retrieval-Augmented LMs
- **Authors:** Akari Asai et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Venue:** arXiv:2411.14199 (Nature correspondence, February 2025)
- **Key Finding:** 8B open-source LM outperforms GPT-4o by 5% and PaperQA2 by 7%; achieves citation accuracy on par with human experts (vs. 78-90% GPT hallucination rate); trained on 45M open-access papers
- **Citation Count:** High (breakthrough citation accuracy)

## Key Systemic Findings Across Papers

### Superhuman Performance Zone (Papers 1-4, 6-7, 14)
- **Ceiling Detection:** Multiple systems (PaperQA2, OpenScholar, otto-SR, AI Scientist-v2) demonstrate superhuman citation accuracy, synthesis, and automation across constrained problem spaces
- **Generalization Bottleneck:** Superhuman performance limited to narrow domains (chemistry synthesis, literature synthesis); open-ended discovery remains at human baseline (ScienceAgentBench: 32.4%)

### Architectural Convergence (Papers 5-13, 24-26)
- **Multi-Agent Consensus:** Successful systems (Robin/FutureHouse, SciAgents, AI Scientist-v2) all employ:
  - Specialized tool-augmented agents (not monolithic LLMs)
  - Knowledge graphs for grounding
  - Agentic RAG for retrieval strategy
  - Self-reflection/verification loops
- **Pattern:** Agentic > Vanilla RAG > Naive prompting across all domains

### Systematic Review Revolution (Papers 14-17)
- **Current SOTA:** otto-SR reproduces 12 Cochrane reviews (12 work-years) in 2 days
- **Human Parity:** Screening (96.7% sensitivity) exceeds traditional dual workflows (81.7%)
- **Remaining Gaps:** Data extraction accuracy good (93.1%); numeric data extraction still problematic; full-pipeline validation needed

### Evaluation Crisis (Papers 27-28)
- **ScienceAgentBench Findings:** Best agent solves only 32.4% of discovery tasks; no agent yet generalizes beyond task-specific optimization
- **Implication:** Current "superhuman" claims apply only to well-defined subtasks; open-ended research discovery remains unsolved

### Literature Review Pipeline Consensus (Papers 1-4, 18-19)
- **Winning Pattern:** Retrieval (semantic + keyword hybrid) → Question-generation (multi-perspective) → Synthesis (self-verification) → Citation grounding
- **Validation:** STORM (25% org improvement), Co-STORM (interactive refinement), OpenScholar (expert parity) all follow this pipeline
- **Gap:** Limited evidence on downstream impact (do generated reviews improve subsequent research decisions?)
